FAMOUS LANDMARKS IN KOLKATA

Chowringhee

Location:Kolkata-700020, West Bengal

A history-rich neighbourhood in Kolkata, Chowringhee attracts countless tourists with its exciting entertainment hotspots and shopping hubs. In the early 17th century, this area was a small village of isolated hovels with tiger-infested jungles, paddy fields, swamps and sporadic huts of fishermen, farmers, woodcutters and weavers in the surroundings. Urbanisation in this region started after the strengthening of British power in the 18th century. The construction of several imposing structures in Chowringhee, in the mid 18th century, earned Kolkata the sobriquet of ‘City of Palaces’.

Mother House

Location:54 A, A.J.C. Bose Road, Taltala, Kolkata-700016

A testament of rock-like faith, indestructible hope and incredible charity – the Mother House of the Missionaries of Charity was home to the Nobel Peace Prize awardee - Mother Teresa. At present, this house is the headquarters of the Missionaries of Charity's international religious congregation. For visitors, only Mother Teresa's Tomb, the museum and her room are open. The tomb, where Mother Teresa was buried on 13th September, 1997 is on the ground floor. People belonging to all creeds come to offer prayers and meditate here.


South Park Street Cemetery

Location:65,Park Street,Mother Teresa Sarani, Kolkata-700016

Established during India's colonial era, South Park Street Cemetery is famed as one of the oldest non-church cemeteries on Earth. You will be amazed to know that in the 19th century, this Christian cemetery was the largest of its kind outside America and Europe. In use since the year 1767 on a land that was previously marshy, this cemetery continued to serve its purpose until 1830. At present, this site is one of Kolkata's important heritage sites and is protected by the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI).


Calcutta High Court

Location:3, Esplanade Row West, Kolkata-700001, West Bengal.

The Calcutta High Court is the oldest High Court in India. It has jurisdiction over the state of West Bengal and the Union Territory of the Andaman and Nicobar Islands. The High Court building's design is based on the Cloth Hall, Ypres, in Belgium.The Calcutta High Court has the distinction of being the first High Court and one of the three Chartered High Courts to be set up in India, along with the High Courts of Bombay, Madras.
